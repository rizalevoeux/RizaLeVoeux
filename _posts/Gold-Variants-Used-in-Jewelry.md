Since ancient times, gold jewelry has existed. This precious metal is widely known in jewelry making because of its beauty, scarcity, and value. However, there are differences among gold jewelry, as different types of gold are used to manufacture various kinds of jewelry. Some information regarding these different types of gold can assist in choosing the best kind of gold.

Pure Gold (24K Gold)

It is the purest form of gold known to man.

24K gold traits:

• Bright yellow color
• Very valuable
• Does not tarnish easily
• Soft metal
• Easy to scratch

Pure gold is extremely soft. Because of its extreme softness, it is not used in jewelry making. To make it more durable and increase its strength, pure gold is mixed with copper, silver, zinc and other metals to create alloys.


22K Gold

22K gold is made up of 91.6% gold and 8.4% of other metals. The addition of these metals gives it strength over pure gold.

Usage:

•	Classic jewelry
•	Wedding pieces
•	Ornaments

22K gold retains its beautiful yellow color but provides increased durability.

18K Gold

18K gold is 75% gold and 25% silver, copper, zinc, or a mix.

Advantages of 18K Gold:

•	More durable compared to 22K or 24K gold
•	Golden-colored metal
•	Durable enough to wear every day
•	Used in the production of fine jewelry

Most luxury jewelry manufacturers utilize 18K gold owing to its advantages.


14K Gold

14K gold is 58.3% gold and 41.7% other metals. It is the most common gold used in jewelry making.

Advantages of 14K Gold:

•	Less expensive than 18K gold
•	Durable
•	Scratch resistant
•	Ideal for daily wear

Gold jewelry also varies according to color.

Yellow Gold

•	Normal appearance of gold
•	Composed of gold, silver, and copper

White Gold

•	Consists of white metals like nickel and palladium
•	Silver-like appearance of gold
•	Normally plated with rhodium for a shiny finish

Rose Gold

•	Made from gold and copper
•	Pink or red color of gold

Conclusion

There are many different forms of gold jewelry that one can wear. It varies in purity, and it varies in color. This makes it clear why people love gold jewelry.
